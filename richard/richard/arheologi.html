NR.1 IN ARCHEOLOGY
We are oldest and largest archaeological organization. We were founded in 1956 by Congress in recognition of its work in developing the Antiquities Act, a cornerstone in the preservation of archaeological sites in the U.S. Today, the Institute has more than 200,000 Members and Subscribers in 107 Local Societies. We promote an informed public interest in the cultures and civilizations of the past, supports archaeological research, fosters the sound professional practice of archaeology and advocates the preservation of the world's archaeological heritage.
We work to provide a unifying voice for the industry that promotes and facilitates knowledge sharing and a common vision; we defines "adventure travel" and establish "best practices" and operating standards; and encourages sustainable and environmentally and culturally sensitive adventure travel practices.
In the last few years, visits to historical sites have ranked third--after dining in restaurants and shopping--among activities undertaken by Americans traveling abroad. The number of people that visit archaeological sites rises every year and the increase in traffic can result in damage to sites. In extreme cases, sites have been closed to the public to prevent further damage.
